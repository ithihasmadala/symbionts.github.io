THE UNITS GAME

Your CDC’s add up to 3+3+4+3+3+3 = 19/25 units. You are left with 6 free units in 2-1. It's recommended to fill these 6 units so that you don’t have extra load during the 3 & 4th year**. Refer to the last page. To utilize them, you can take Humanities electives (HuEls or HELs), Disciplinary Electives (DELs) and project courses (SOPs, LOPs, DOPs). You can even leave one slot free if you are writing many compres for 1-2.

Some of the possible combinations:

  • HEL  x 2 - One of the most popular options. 
  • 1 HEL + 1 DEL - The second most popular option.
  • 1 HEL + 1 PROJECT COURSE 
  • 1 DEL + 1 PROJECT COURSE

**For your degree completion ( both the degrees), apart from the CDCs, you are required to complete a certain number of electives: 3 HELs + 5 Bio DELs + (X) 2nd-degree DELs.

HUMANITIES ELECTIVES

These courses are a bit different than CDCs/DELs. They are pretty easy to score and students generally get a good grade. Some HELs are extremely lite and the prof is extremely lenient in grading. Hence, students with good PR numbers will prefer these courses. If let’s say you get a bad PR and you want a certain HEL, after registration, you may request the respective IC for that course to add a seat, and later during the add/swap window, you may be able to take that course. Make sure you attend all the classes if you are stuck in such a situation. But keep in mind, IC will only increase a limited number of seats, and there’ll be huge competition for good HELs, so finally it will all be a game of luck during the add/swap window.


BIO DISCIPLINARY ELECTIVES

There aren't many DELs offered during 2-1 because most of them have your 2-1 courses as prereqs. You have one option:

Water Sanitation:  All learning is done from online videos (4 Coursera courses), a quick way to go through the videos is to download and read the subtitle files. Lite course and is a prerequisite to the Water Sanitation lab. It is completely open laptop (midsem and compre) and in addition to this there are 4 MCQ based quizzes that are closed book (10-15 marks each). If taken seriously, you can land up with great internships abroad with relatively less effort due to the demand this niche has.


PROJECT COURSES

Apart from these courses, Project courses in your own branch are also considered as DELs. Project courses include:  Study Oriented Project [SOP], Lab Oriented Project [LOP] and Design Oriented Project [DOP].

Project courses are different from your regular courses in the sense that they do not have a written midsem & compre exam. Neither do they have quizzes. However, the prof may ask you to make presentations and a final report submission is compulsory. The prof will judge you based on your work during the semester and accordingly grade. The profs generally give an ‘A’ grade if you do your work sincerely.  

To register for a project course, you need to talk to the prof under whom you want to do a project course, in the previous sem. For example, if you plan to do a project course in 2-2, you will need to ask the prof in 2-1 and confirm that he/she will give you a project course under him/her. At the end of 2-1, you’ll receive a google form wherein you are supposed to add the details of this project course. So, plan accordingly if you wish to take a project course in any semester. 

Bio 2nd year is the best year you will have in the 4 years on campus. Participate in extracurricular activities, explore Goa (hopefully in 2-2) and have fun studying with the best profs on campus!
